## 1. Director

**Overview:** The Director provides overall leadership, vision, and accountability for the Undergraduate Research Conference (URC). This role ensures that the conference remains aligned with its mission of expanding equitable access to undergraduate research presentation and professional development opportunities.

**Core Responsibilities:**

- Set strategic priorities and oversee the full lifecycle of the conference from planning through post-event evaluation.
- Manage Executive Committee and meetings; set agendas and ensure follow-through on action items.
- Serve as the primary liaison to faculty advisors, Campus Y, the Office of Undergraduate Research, and other institutional partners.
- Coordinate Internal and External Operations leads to ensure timelines, budgets, and deliverables remain aligned.
- Oversee risk management, conflict resolution, and major decision-making.
- Represent the URC to sponsors, university leadership, and external stakeholders.

---

## 2. Internal Operations

### a. Finance Committee

The Finance Committee ensures financial longevity and realistic event planning.

**Core Responsibilities:**

- Develop and manage the comprehensive conference budget.
- Track expenses, income, and reimbursements throughout the planning cycle.
- Coordinate with granting bodies to ensure compliance.
- Monitor grant and sponsorship funds in collaboration with Grant Writing and External Operations.
- Prepare financial summaries for advisors, sponsors, and post-conference reporting.

### b. Logistics

**Overview:** Logistics is responsible for the physical, technical, and operational infrastructure of the conference.

**Core Responsibilities:**

- Reserve and coordinate university venues (e.g., Great Hall, Union, Gillings, Alumni Center).
- Manage room assignments, spacing, accessibility, and audiovisual requirements.
- Coordinate signage, poster boards, supplies, and equipment ordering.
- Liaise with facilities, catering, and AV services.
- Oversee setup, breakdown, and troubleshooting during conference days.

### c. Conference Experience

**Overview:** Conference Experience focuses on attendee satisfaction, inclusivity, and professional development opportunities.

**Core Responsibilities:**

- Design the registration, check-in, and wayfinding experience.
- Coordinate hospitality, catering logistics, and volunteer staffing.
- Plan pre-conference events to prepare attendees.
- Train volunteers to support sessions, speakers, and presenters.
- Ensure presenters, speakers, and sponsors are supported throughout the event.
- Develop and distribute post-conference surveys to presenters, speakers, sponsors, and advisors.
- Compile feedback to inform evaluation and future improvements.

## 3. External Operations

### a. Grant Writing

**Overview:** The Grant Writing team secures financial support from institutional and external funding sources.

**Core Responsibilities:**

- Identify grant opportunities aligned with URC's mission.
- Draft proposals, budgets, and impact statements.
- Coordinate submissions with Finance and Director.
- Track deadlines, reporting requirements, and outcomes.

### b. Public Relations

**Overview:** Public Relations manages official communications and institutional visibility.

**Core Responsibilities:**

- Coordinate with University PR and external news sources.
- Draft announcements, press releases, and newsletters.
- Manage email communications to participants and partners.
- Ensure messaging reflects the conference's equity and access goals.
- Develop a long-term social media strategy and posting calendar.

- Promote calls for abstracts, speakers, and sponsors.
- Provide live updates during the conference and post-event highlights.

### c. Web Development

**Overview:** Web Development manages the design, functionality, and maintenance of the URC website.

**Core Responsibilities:**

- Design and launch the conference website by April.
- Maintain updated content including schedules, submissions, and sponsors.
- Ensure accessibility, usability, and consistency with branding.
- Coordinate updates with PR, Recruitment, and Director.

## 4. Recruitment

### a. Speaker Recruitment

**Overview:** Speaker Recruitment secures professional development and keynote speakers.

**Core Responsibilities:**

- Identify speakers from academia, industry, and sponsoring organizations.
- Manage invitations, confirmations, and scheduling.
- Coordinate speaker logistics, honoraria, and recognition.
- Ensure speaker content aligns with conference goals.

### b. Attendee Recruitment

**Overview:** Attendee Recruitment manages the abstract submission and selection process.

**Core Responsibilities:**

- Launch and manage the call for abstracts.
- Conduct targeted outreach to diverse student populations.
- Coordinate abstract review and acceptance decisions.
- Communicate presentation expectations, schedules, and logistics to presenters.
